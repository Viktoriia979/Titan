Titan:

Titan is the largest natural satellite of Saturn and the second-largest moon in the Solar System. Scientists study Titan because it is the only moon with a thick atmosphere and has rivers, lakes, and seas made of liquid methane and ethane instead of water. Studying Titan helps scientists understand how planets and moons form and may provide clues about how life could develop in differen

Scientists believe Titan formed about 4.5 billion years ago from the cloud of gas, dust, and ice that surrounded Saturn as the planet was forming. Gravity pulled these materials together over millions of years to create the moon. Titan is made mostly of rock and water ice, with a dense atmosphere made mainly of nitrogen, along with small amounts of methane and other gases.

Titan's surface is covered with icy plains, rocky areas, sand dunes, and lakes and seas of liquid methane and ethane. Thick orange clouds in its atmosphere make it difficult to see the surface from space. Titan has a diameter of about 5,150 kilometres, making it larger than the planet Mercury. It orbits Saturn at a distance of about 1.2 million kilometres and takes about 16 Earth days to complete one orbit.
